# Product

## Register

product

## Users

LibreTutor is for individual learners who study from long-form material: books, PDFs, EPUBs, course notes, and Markdown manuscripts. They are usually reading alone, in a focused session, and want a private tutor that can stay with one source over many lessons.

The user is not browsing marketing content. They are inside a learning workspace, trying to understand one lesson, answer questions, recover from confusion, and keep enough continuity to return later.

## Product Purpose

LibreTutor turns a single source file into a guided learning path. It builds a course tree, opens each lesson as a tutor dialogue, checks coverage, generates tailored exercises, grades answers, and keeps a tutor diary as a memory of the course.

Success means the learner feels oriented, accompanied, and able to keep moving through a book without the course becoming endless or fragmented.

## Brand Personality

Attentive, literary, restrained.

The interface should feel like a quiet study desk with a skilled tutor nearby. It can be warm and humane, but it should not become decorative, childish, or theatrical. The product voice should be precise, gentle, and concrete.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with bright metric cards and decorative gradients.
- Developer-facing copy, test traces, debug labels, or implementation language in the user experience.
- Chat UI that looks like an anonymous support widget instead of a learning conversation.
- Overly playful tutor visuals that reduce the seriousness of study.
- Dense analytical dashboards that replace the tutor diary's narrative memory.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the learner oriented: show where they are, what the current lesson is doing, and what action comes next.
2. Let the tutor feel present: the teacher side of the conversation should have a distinct voice, avatar, and pacing without stealing focus.
3. Preserve reading calm: typography, spacing, and color should support long sessions and source-grounded thinking.
4. Use progress sparingly: surface status when it helps action, not as a dashboard of pressure.
5. Respect attempts as learning moments: retry, assessment, exercises, and diary entries should feel like part of one humane loop.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve keyboard focus states, 44px touch targets on mobile, readable text at small widths, and reduced-motion alternatives for nonessential animation. Do not encode state with color alone.
